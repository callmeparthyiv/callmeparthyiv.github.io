Program Curriculum

Msc. Computer Sciene - Data Science program at University of Regina is designed to equip students with skills required to turn data into knowlegde, and knowlegde into values. The program is highly focused and aligned with data science. The program covers key concepts about data visualization, statistical analysis, machine learning, artificial intelligence, big data technologies. Students will have a great opportunity to explore and have hands on exposure to industry standard problems as well as real world challenges. The program enables students to provide innovative solutions for complex problems existing in the world.


Program Requirements

The program length is 18-24 months. Students have to take 4 required courses, 2 elective courses, complete the seminar. Students have to participate in Co-op Education program for minimum 2 terms and after completion of co-op education. After completion of all required & elective courses they have to work on a standlone project. The project work must be completed within 1 term. Students have to present oral presentation in front of departmental committee and submit a technical report.


Required Courses

Data Science Foundation
Broad overview of the data science process lifecycle and methods. Topics include data ethics, data discovery, data preparation, model planning, machine learning model implementation, and evaluation, visualization, and delivery.
Probability and Statistics
Statistical basis for machine learning. Topics include distributions, probabilities, sampling, hypothesis testing, Bayesâ€™ theorem, maximum likelihood, machine learning theory
Artificial Intelligence & Machine Learning
Machine learning approaches applied to real-world problems. Topics include classification, regression, clustering, decision trees and random forests, Bayesian networks, deep learning, face and object recognition, time-series forecasting, anomaly detection, natural language processing, and machine translation.
Big Data Management
An introductory course to managing and processing large datasets using modern technologies such as Hadoop, Spark, and NoSQL databases. Topics on data storage, retrieval, and analysis techniques essential for handling big data in real-world applications.

Elective Courses

Advance Data Visualization
Advanced course on visualization to make students familiar with visualization libraries such as seaborn, matplotlib etc. Topics on graphs, charts, maps as well as a project on data visulizaiton using Power BI or Tableau.
Deep Learning & Health Informatics
The course focuses on the application of deep learning techniques in healthcare and medical data analysis. Topics include convolutional neural networks (CNNs), and recurrent neural networks (RNNs) for tasks like medical image analysis, disease prediction.
Financial Market Analytics
The course focuses on in-depth understanding of Financial market as well as analysis of stocks and bonds. Topics include application of statistical methods, artificial intelligence techniques to forecast market trends, evalute risks inorder to make informed data driven decision.
Speech, Natural Language Processing and the Web
The course explores the foundations of speech recognition and natural language processing (NLP) with a focus on web-based applications. Students will learn techniques for language modeling, text processing, sentiment analysis. The course also covers the integration of NLP technologies into web platforms, search engines, and voice assistants.

Seminar

Communication & Ethics in Data Science
Data Science students will attend a professionally focused seminar series with topics including ethics, privacy, innovation, and effective communication.

Project & Co-op Education

Project & Co-op Education is a mandatory requirement for graduation. Students are required to maintain more than 80 GPA throughout the course work. In order to be eligible for Co-op Education students are required to complete any two required courses and one elective course with overall 85 GPA. Students are considered eligible as soon as they complete the first semester.
